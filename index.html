About the Author

Some towns keep secrets better than people do

Harper Callahan writes atmospheric Southern mysteries from her home on the North Carolina coast, where she splits her time between writing, sea-glass hunting, and trying to keep her rescue dog from stealing sandwiches off the porch.

She loves weather, good coffee, and the kind of story that leaves you a little uneasy about the house you're reading in.

The Lantern Bay Mysteries is her first series.

The Series

The Lantern Bay Mysteries

Standalone murder mysteries set on a barrier island off the North Carolina coast. One woman, one town, and the slow unraveling of a secret that's older than both.

Book One

The Tide Remembers

Nine months into her new life on Heron Island, Nell Whitaker finds a body in the tidal pools behind her café. Avery Cole was twenty-nine, engaged, and the loudest voice against a resort development threatening the island's protected marsh. Everyone has a theory. Most of them are wrong.

Nell was an investigative journalist in another life — before a story in Washington got a source killed and she ran south to a building on the corner of Front and Pelican that she turned into the Compass Rose. She was trying to disappear. Lantern Bay had other plans.

Book Two

What the Harbor Knows

She vanished from a yacht at the height of the island's biggest party. Her cocktail glass was still sweating on the rail. Her shoes were lined up at the swim platform. Nobody saw her go.

When Catherine Aldridge disappears during the Driftmere Regatta, Nell is pulled into a case that runs from old-money politics to a decades-old land trust, a trail of forged documents, and a man in a dark sedan who has been watching the Compass Rose — and who has nothing to do with Catherine Aldridge at all.

Book Three

The House on Dune Lane

A fire at an empty mansion. A body in a wall. Forty years of silence.

When the old Ballard house on Dune Lane burns, firefighters find skeletal remains sealed inside the chimney — a young woman who disappeared in 1984 and an island that agreed not to ask why. The killer is dead. But the people who knew, the people who suspected, the people who chose the silence that let a woman vanish from a community of twenty-eight hundred without a single question asked — they're still here.

Book Four

The Lantern on the Hill

The archivist knew the deed transfers didn't add up. She was right. It cost her everything.

When the town's archivist is found dead in a locked cottage on the island's highest hill, Nell follows a paper trail from shell companies to a network she thought she'd left behind in Washington. For the first time, the island mystery and the secret she's been running from are the same story.

Book Five

Salt and Shadow

The storm is coming. So is the truth.

A hurricane hits Heron Island. When the water recedes, there's a body in the harbor — a man who didn't drown. Someone came by boat, in a Category 3, to make sure he never talked. And Nell Whitaker's trip back to Washington has just connected every thread she's been pulling for four years.

In Development

Book Six (Series Finale)

The Watch Hill

He arrived by helicopter. He ordered a cortado. He knew her name.

The man behind the network — behind the shell companies, the killings, four years of shadows — has come to Heron Island. When someone close to Nell is hurt, she makes the choice she's been carrying since Washington. She writes the story. The reckoning is here. The light is coming back on.
